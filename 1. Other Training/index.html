Neil Armstrong


Neil Armstrong is most famous for being the first man to walk on the moon. Throughout his life he accomplished many things such as being a aerospace engineer, naval aviator, test pilot and university professor.

Timeline/Achievements

  • August 5, 1930 Born in Wapakoneta, Ohio
  • July 20, 1936 - At the age of five, Neil Armstrong took his first plane ride in a Ford Trimotor.
  • At 16 years old, Armstrong received his student flight license and completed his first solo flight before he received his drivers license.
  • Armstrong was active in the Boy Scouts. One of the personal items that he brought with him on the Apollo 11 flight was his World Scout Badge
  • In 1947, Armstrong began studying aeronautical engineering at Purdue University
  • Armstrong's education was paid for by the Navy's Holloway Plan and on January 26, 1949 Armstrong was called to to the service. He reported to the Naval Air Station Pensacola for flight training. On August 6, 1950 Armstrong received a letter informing him that he was a fully qualified Naval Aviator.
  • Armstrong flew 78 missions over Korea during the Korean War
  • Education: In 1955 Armstrong graduated with a Bachelor of Science in Aeronautical Engineering. In 1970 he complete his Master of Science in Aerospace Engineering at the University of Southern California.
  • In March 1955 began his work as a test pilot.
  • On August 15, 1957 Armstrong flew for the first time in a rocket plane, the Bell X-1B
  • In 1958 Armstrong was selected for the US Air Force's Man in Space Soonest program
  • On December 23, 1968 Armstrong was offered to be the commander of Apollo 11. He turned it down in favor of Buzz Aldrin.
  • Apollo 11 landed on the moon on July 20, 1969.
  • Upon landing on the moon, Armstrong says the now famous phrase, "That's one small step for (a) man; one giant leap for mankind".
  • In 1979 Armstrong became a University Professor of Aerospace Engineering. He resigned from teaching in 1979.
  • Armstrong died on August 25, 2012 due to complications with vascular bypass surgery.
